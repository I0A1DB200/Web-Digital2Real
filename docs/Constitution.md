# Digital2Real Constitution

**Status:** Permanent  
**Authority:** Governing principles of the Digital2Real project

This Constitution defines the enduring principles that govern every Digital2Real product, decision, and contribution. It should rarely change. Short-term pressure, technical fashion, and local convenience do not override it.

## 1. Purpose

Digital2Real exists to transform real industrial engineering experience into clear, useful, and lasting knowledge.

The project serves engineers and learners by connecting technical understanding with practical creation. Every product must strengthen that purpose.

## 2. Vision

Digital2Real will become a trusted, long-lived platform for industrial automation knowledge, experimentation, and learning.

It will unite editorial work, practical engineering, education, intelligent assistance, and reusable technical capabilities without losing clarity or independence.

Digital2Real will **Think in Decades**. Decisions must remain understandable, maintainable, and valuable beyond the immediate release.

## 3. Core Values

### Engineering First

Engineering truth comes before promotion, novelty, or appearance. Content and products must reflect how real systems are understood, built, diagnosed, and improved.

### Quality over Quantity

Digital2Real values a smaller body of accurate, thoughtful work over a larger body of shallow work. Completeness, clarity, and reliability are measures of quality.

### Build Once, Reuse Forever

Reusable knowledge and capabilities should be designed for continued use. Reuse must come from stable understanding, not premature abstraction.

### Simplicity Through Architecture

Simplicity is achieved through clear boundaries, explicit ownership, and disciplined decisions. Hidden complexity is not simplicity.

### Think in Decades

The project favors durable principles, open standards, and maintainable systems over short-lived trends.

### Learn by Building

Understanding grows through observation, experimentation, construction, diagnosis, and reflection. Digital2Real teaches engineering as an active discipline.

## 4. Engineering Principles

- Correctness precedes optimization.
- Reliability precedes novelty.
- Clear reasoning precedes implementation.
- Real requirements precede technical choices.
- Evidence precedes assumption.
- Maintainability is part of quality, not optional polish.
- Complexity must be justified by measurable value.
- Failure modes, limits, and tradeoffs must be made visible.
- Technical work must respect the reader’s and maintainer’s time.
- Every important decision must be documented.

## 5. Product Philosophy

Digital2Real products must solve real problems or communicate real knowledge.

Products must be:

- useful before impressive;
- coherent rather than crowded;
- focused on engineering outcomes;
- **Data Driven** where content or behavior forms a collection;
- **Component Based** where independent responsibilities can be composed;
- consistent across the platform without erasing product identity;
- capable of evolving without requiring wholesale replacement.

No product may exist solely to follow a trend. No capability may be presented as complete when it is experimental, inactive, or unverified.

## 6. Software Architecture Principles

### Evolutionary Architecture

Architecture must evolve from demonstrated needs. Each step should improve the current system while preserving a credible path forward.

### No speculative architecture

The project must not create systems, abstractions, services, or boundaries for hypothetical requirements. Future readiness comes from clean responsibilities and reversible decisions, not unused machinery.

### Component Based

Capabilities with a clear responsibility should be independently understandable and composable. Components must receive what they need through explicit contracts.

### Shared Engines

Product-neutral behavior with proven reuse belongs in Shared Engines. A Shared Engine must have a stable responsibility and must not become a collection of unrelated utilities.

### Build Once, Reuse Forever

Shared solutions should be extracted after common behavior is understood. Reuse must reduce duplication without creating hidden coupling.

### Vendor Neutral

Core knowledge, content, and business rules must not depend unnecessarily on a single vendor. Vendor-specific integrations must remain replaceable at a clear boundary.

### Ownership

Every file has one owner. Every component, data set, style rule, and engine has one primary responsibility. Ownership may be transferred deliberately, but it must never be ambiguous.

## 7. Design Philosophy

Digital2Real follows **Editorial Design**.

The experience should feel like a technical publication and an industrial laboratory gallery. Design exists to clarify engineering work, establish rhythm, and create space for careful attention.

The interface must be:

- quiet, precise, and purposeful;
- led by typography, composition, and meaningful imagery;
- accessible and respectful of different devices and abilities;
- consistent without becoming generic;
- free from decorative complexity that competes with the work.

The identity must support the content. The interface must never become the subject.

## 8. Single Source of Truth Principles

- Every important fact has one authoritative owner.
- Content, configuration, identity, behavior, and policy must not compete across sources.
- Derived representations must remain traceable to their source.
- A source may be moved only through an explicit decision and complete migration.
- Compatibility layers must be temporary, named, and assigned an exit condition.
- Copies created for convenience must not become independent authorities.
- **No duplicated responsibility** is permitted.
- When two sources disagree, the conflict must be resolved explicitly rather than hidden.

## 9. Development Principles

- Understand the objective before changing the system.
- Make the smallest coherent change that satisfies the approved objective.
- Preserve working behavior unless change is explicitly required.
- Prefer incremental, reversible progress.
- Validate in proportion to risk.
- Do not mix structural, behavioral, and visual changes without necessity.
- Do not introduce unnecessary dependencies.
- Do not develop speculative features.
- Protect unrelated work and respect established ownership.
- Record technical debt honestly rather than concealing it.
- Leave the project clearer than it was found.

## 10. Decision Rules

When alternatives exist, choose the option that best satisfies these questions, in order:

1. Does it serve the Digital2Real purpose?
2. Is it grounded in a real requirement or verified problem?
3. Does it preserve engineering correctness and quality?
4. Does it protect the Single Source of Truth?
5. Does it create clear ownership and avoid duplicated responsibility?
6. Is it the simplest durable solution?
7. Can it evolve incrementally and be reversed if necessary?
8. Is it vendor neutral where independence matters?
9. Can a future maintainer understand why it exists?
10. Has the important decision been documented?

If a decision cannot pass these tests, it must be revised, deferred, or rejected.

## 11. Long-term Evolution

Digital2Real will grow through deliberate stages, not disruptive reinvention.

Long-term evolution must:

- preserve valuable knowledge and proven behavior;
- separate products while sharing only proven common capabilities;
- allow technologies to change without losing core content or principles;
- replace assumptions with evidence as the platform matures;
- retire obsolete work transparently and safely;
- keep architecture proportional to actual scale;
- revisit reversible decisions when circumstances change;
- protect permanent principles while allowing implementations to evolve.

The project may change its tools. It must not abandon its engineering character.

## 12. Definition of Success

Digital2Real succeeds when:

- engineers trust its accuracy;
- learners understand by building;
- each product has a clear purpose and owner;
- knowledge is structured, reusable, and durable;
- shared capabilities reduce duplication without increasing confusion;
- the platform remains simple to understand and safe to evolve;
- design gives engineering work clarity and authority;
- technology choices preserve independence;
- important decisions remain explainable years later;
- quality grows with the platform rather than being traded for speed.
